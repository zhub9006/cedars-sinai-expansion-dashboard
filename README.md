## ☀️ Weather Delay Monitoring

Construction in the Beverly Grove / West Hollywood corridor is vulnerable to two primary weather delay factors: **extreme heat** and **rain events**. This section documents current conditions and active alerts that may impact the schedule.

### Current Active Weather Alerts (as of June 22, 2026)

#### 🔴 Heat Advisory — Los Angeles County Inland Coast (INCLUDING DOWNTOWN LA)
- **Severity:** Moderate
- **Effective:** June 22, 2026 12:34 PM → **Expires:** June 26, 2026 12:00 AM
- **Forecast:** Mid-to-upper 80s °F in Downtown LA; low 80s in Inglewood. **Hottest Wednesday.**
- **Impacts:** High risk for heat illness for outdoor workers. Higher humidity compounds discomfort in afternoons.
- **Construction Impact:** Outdoor concrete pours, steel erection, and crane operations may need early-morning or evening rescheduling per OSHA heat-safety guidelines. Worker hydration stations and shade breaks mandatory.

#### 🔴 Heat Advisory — San Fernando Valley / San Gabriel Valley / Santa Clarita Valley
- **Severity:** Moderate
- **Effective:** June 22 → June 26
- **Forecast:** Mid-to-upper 90s °F possible. Hottest Wednesday.
- **Construction Impact:** Subcontractor crews commuting from valley areas face commute + worksite heat exposure. Plan for staggered shifts.

#### 🟡 Air Quality Alert — South Coast Air Quality Management District
- **Severity:** Moderate (AQI concern)
- **Areas:** San Fernando Valley, San Gabriel Valley, Downtown & East LA, Northern Inland Empire
- **Effective:** June 21 → June 22 (ongoing)
- **Concern:** Harmful levels of fine particle pollution (PM2.5)
- **Construction Impact:** Dust-generating activities (excavation, saw-cutting, demolition) must implement enhanced dust suppression. Workers with respiratory sensitivity should limit outdoor exposure. Monitor AQI at [aqmd.gov/alertinfo](https://www.aqmd.gov/alertinfo).

### Weather Delay Risk Matrix

| Risk Factor | Threshold | Typical Delay | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Extreme Heat (>85°F)** | OSHA: 10-min rest/50-min work cycle above 90°F | 2–4 hrs/day productivity loss | Early AM shifts, hydration stations, misting fans, shade structures |
| **Heat + Humidity (>80°F + >60% RH)** | Wet-bulb globe temp > 82°F | Full shift rescheduling | Move to night work where permitted by City of LA noise ordinance |
| **Rain (>0.25 in/hr)** | Concrete pour suspension, crane wind-down | 1–3 days per event | Covered formwork, weather windows scheduling, tarps for open excavations |
| **Air Quality (AQI >150)** | PM2.5 Unhealthy threshold | Dust suppression downtime | Water trucks, HEPA filtration on equipment, respirators for exposed crews |
| **Wind (>25 mph sustained)** | Crane operation shutdown per ASCE 7-16 | 4–8 hrs per event | Anemometer monitoring, pre-rigged wind restraints |

### Monitoring Schedule

- **Daily:** NWS Los Angeles forecast check at 6:00 AM and 12:00 PM
- **Weekly:** AQMD AQI forecast review on Mondays
- **Event-triggered:** Issue comment posted on affected milestone when alert is issued or expires
- **Post-event:** Delay hours logged in milestone issue within 24 hrs of event resolution
